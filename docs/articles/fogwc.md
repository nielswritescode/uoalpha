## Questioning the existing system

- FWC is a societal taboo

> Just try advertising a massage studio with 'UNCERTIFIED, BUT VERY EXPERIENCED MASSAGE PROFESSIONAL' and you'll encounter the taboo. 

- Discussing FWC is a societal taboo

> Try telling a yoga teacher you teach without credentials and you'll quickly hit a trigger.

- Is it likely that, given the taboo state of FWC, it will be rejected for non-objective reasons?

- How is the ego, survival instict and desire of current teachers with credentials linked to the possibility of discussing FWC?


## Facilitation as a range
We hypothesize that facilitation without credentials could be an important tool for UO. To understand how this applies, we need to understand facilitation as a range. 

Let's take the traditional yoga teacher role, and transform it into a range:

- Yoga teacher
- Yoga facilitator
- Yoga spaceholder

The **yoga teacher** might explain the poses and correct students. The **yoga facilitator** might simply list poses. The **yoga spaceholder** might just let the people into the class, arrange yoga mats and make tea. 

It becomes easier to see how a spaceholder might not need credentials. Let's increase the range even more:

- Yoga teacher+
- Yoga teacher
- Yoga facilitator++
- Yoga facilitator+
- Yoga facilitator
- Yoga spaceholder

Someone with no credentials, but a lot of experience might be able to fulfill the facilitator++ role. It will depend on the person and context. This range allows us to shift from a traditional view 'You shouldn't teach yoga without credentials.' to a more flexible view 'On the scale from spaceholder to teacher, what can this person facilitate?'
