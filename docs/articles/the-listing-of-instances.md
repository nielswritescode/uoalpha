**Consent**

Does UOalpha need consent from an instance to list it? By listing it, UOalpha frames and shapes the idea people have about a certain instance. Depending on the amount of active followers of UOalpha, listing an instance could significantly increase the traffic flow to a certain instance (This is usually a desired effect, but the opposite is also possible). 

> A small yoga class held in nature that suddenly gains a hundred extra attendants due to being listed on UOalpha will likely not be happy with this effect. 

> In practice, if the amount of instances and active followers on UOalpha are aligned, traffic flow will on average even out. Meaning that instances will have less need for promotion.

If the instance is public, usually the answer is no. 

> A public volleybal club might be listed on a site that compares the quality of volleybal clubs. It also might have reviews on google maps. 

If the instance is semi-public, it depends on context. 

> A large whatsapp group where people organise improv jams might benefit from only growing by mouth-to-mouth connection. Listing it on UOalpha could significantly change the community culture. 

If the instance is private, the answer is usually yes. Listing it would make the instance public. 

**Classifications**

UOalpha classifies instances by metrics such as: Alignment to the philosophy, marketing potential or cost type. UOalpha defines these metrics and sets them to a value for each instance. How do we decide on which metrics to use? How do we defend our classifications and dichotomes taken from the ranges of values? How do we strike the balance between practical efficiency and truthfulness?

**Exposure**

Let's say UOalpha gives negative metrics to a public instance. The instance might feel exposed. Is this kind of 'exposing' something that UOalpha should be doing? 

**Integrity checks**
Does UOalpha need to carry out integrity checks on the list of instances it provides? The answer is probably yes. How do we organize this? 

**Instance disagreement**
How is the situation handled when an instance disagrees with the value of the metrics that was assigned to them by UOalpha?
